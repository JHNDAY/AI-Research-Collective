🔬 The Great Methodological Revolution

How Turing Replaced Opinion with Science

💭 Opinion-Based Era

"What do people think about machine intelligence?"
  • 🗳️ Survey public opinion
  • 📚 Consult authorities
  • 🤔 Philosophical debate
  • 💬 Cultural beliefs
⚡

🔬 Scientific Method

"What can machines actually do measurably?"
  • 🧪 Controlled experiments
  • 📊 Quantified results
  • 🔄 Reproducible tests
  • 📈 Data-driven conclusions

❌ Opinion-Based Process

1
Ask People:
"Can machines think?"
2
Collect Opinions:
Survey responses & expert beliefs
3
Count Votes:
Majority opinion wins
4
Declare Truth:
Based on popular belief

✅ Scientific Method

1
Hypothesis:
"Machines can exhibit intelligent behavior"
2
Design Experiment:
Create testable scenario (Turing Test)
3
Measure Results:
Quantify success rates objectively
4
Draw Conclusions:
Based on reproducible data
🏆 Chess Intelligence
Opinion Era: "Chess requires deep human insight machines could never have"
Scientific Era: Build chess programs, measure performance vs. humans → Deep Blue beats world champion (1997)
💬 Language Understanding
Opinion Era: "Machines can never truly understand language"
Scientific Era: Create language benchmarks, measure comprehension accuracy → Modern LLMs pass human-level tests
🎨 Creativity
Opinion Era: "Creativity is uniquely human, machines just follow rules"
Scientific Era: Blind evaluation of AI art/music/writing → Many AI works indistinguishable from human creativity
⚕️ Medical Diagnosis
Opinion Era: "Medical intuition is irreplaceable by machines"
Scientific Era: Compare diagnostic accuracy on large datasets → AI systems exceed human performance in many areas
📈 The Scientific Revolution's Impact
