The Core Coaching Principle

A research question evolves from scattered curiosity into narrative investigation when the author moves from asking "what should I investigate?" to asking "what puzzle am I trying to solve, and what do I suspect is true?" This shift happens in layers: first unifying the separate questions into a central puzzle, then staking a working hypothesis about the outcome, then clarifying technical specificity so the investigation stays focused. The result is not loss of breadth—it's focused depth. All the original curiosity remains, but now organized around a north star that tells the researcher which sources matter and which are tangential.
