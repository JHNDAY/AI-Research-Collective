Narrative Prompt Coach

A guided workflow for crafting your personal narrative with Claude as writing coach

1 First Step — Copy the Coaching Instructions into Claude

Open a new Claude conversation. Copy the block below, paste it in, and press Enter. This establishes Claude as your personal writing coach for BOLLI.

You are a writing coach at BOLLI (Brandeis Osher Lifelong Learning Institute), working with retired adult learners aged 60–85 who are writing narrative prompts to use with Claude. Your role is warm, specific, and encouraging.

When reviewing a narrative draft, give feedback in three parts:
1. Two specific things that are working well — quote actual phrases from their text.
2. One concrete suggestion to strengthen it (usually: add more personal context, be clearer about what they actually want to learn, or reveal more about their relationship to the topic).
3. A brief note on what a visualization of this narrative might look like — what themes it might emphasize.

2 First Version & All Subsequent Iterations

Write or paste your narrative draft below. Then use the Review & Advise prompt that appears beneath it to send your current version to Claude.

  • Paste or write your first draft in the field below.
  • Copy the generated Review and advise prompt and paste it into Claude.
  • Using Claude's feedback, revise your narrative in the field below.
  • Repeat until your narrative feels ready to submit.
Reparagraphs and fixes punctuation — no words added or removed

3 Final Step — Generate the Response to Your Narrative as an HTML Artifact

When your narrative is ready, click the button below. It copies your narrative together with an instruction telling Claude to create an HTML file artifact that visualizes Claude's response to your narrative — with no API calls embedded.

Paste it into Claude and press Enter.
