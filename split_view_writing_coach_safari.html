Split-Screen Setup

Running the Writing Coach side-by-side with Claude

The Writing Coach works best when you can see the coaching instructions and your Claude conversation at the same time — one to copy from, one to paste into. Split screen does exactly that: two windows, side by side, no toggling back and forth and losing your place.

It takes about two minutes to set up the first time. Once you've done it, you'll use the same arrangement for any session material in AILit.

Before you start

  • The AILit portal open in your browser
  • A Mac running a recent version of macOS (Windows users — see the note in Step 4)
  • About two minutes
1

Start at home base

Open the AILit Portal

Begin at your AILit portal page. This is the hub for every session — chat links to Claude, ChatGPT, and Gemini along the bottom, plus your writing tools and the current session's materials above.

The AILit portal — your hub for every session's tools and chat links.

2

Find the right tool

Open the Writing Coach

Scroll to that session's Session Materials and click Writing Coach. This opens the Narrative Prompt Coach page — the one with the Coaching Instructions block you'll be handing to Claude in a moment.

Session Materials — Writing Coach is one of five tools for this session.

3

The step people skip

Open Claude in Its Own Window

Back on the portal, find the Claude link under Chat with AI. Right-click it (Control-click on a trackpad) and choose Open Link in New Window — not New Tab.

This matters: split screen arranges separate windows. Two tabs in the same window can't be split apart.

Right-click the Claude link → Open Link in New Window.

4

Right after it opens

Claude, Ready and Waiting

In its new window, Claude opens fresh — "Welcome, John" — model set to Sonnet 4.6, cursor sitting in the message box, ready for input.

Claude opens fresh in its own window, ready for the coaching instructions.

5

The macOS shortcut

Snap the Windows

On either window, hover your mouse over the green button in the top-left corner — don't click it, just hover. A small menu appears. Under Move & Resize, choose the left-half tile.

Repeat on the second window and choose the right-half tile instead.

Hover the green button (top-left) to reveal Move & Resize.

On Windows instead Drag a window to the left or right edge of the screen (or press Windows key + Left Arrow / Right Arrow). Click the second window from the thumbnail grid that appears to fill the other half.
6

The payoff

The Finished Setup

This is the view you're after: the Narrative Prompt Coach instructions on the left, Claude open and waiting on the right.

Select and copy the Coaching Instructions block on the left, click into Claude's message box on the right, paste, and press Enter. Claude is now set up as your personal writing coach for the session.

Coaching Instructions on the left, Claude on the right — ready to paste.

One arrangement, every session

Once macOS has paired these two windows, clicking either one will usually offer to bring its partner along — so next time, getting back to this split-screen view takes a click, not seven steps.
