AI-Assisted Course Development  ·  What We Built & Where We Go Next
Dear SGL AI Cohort,
This note outlines the path forward from our February workshop on integrating AI into the Study Group Leader course development process.
What We Set Out to Do

Three Core Objectives

The workshop was designed to enable each SGL to use AI across three dimensions of course work:

1
Define a Course Syllabus
Transform your personal passion and expertise into a structured, week-by-week course outline.
2
Research Important Topics
Use AI to explore and deepen the subject matter that forms the backbone of your syllabus.
3
Create Course Materials
Produce polished, ready-to-use materials for administering your course in the classroom.
Our Major Accomplishment

Course narratives produced for more than half (see Course Narratives) of workshop participants — a strong foundation to build on as we move to syllabus and materials.

Immediate Next Steps

From Narrative to Syllabus

The work now moves from narrative to execution. Our next two tasks carry us from story to structured course:

1
Create a Syllabus from the Narrative
Transform your personal course story into a structured, week-by-week syllabus with AI as your collaborator.
2
Visualize the Syllabus
Produce an interactive, shareable visualization — a format that can be used directly to administer the course.
Integrated Throughout

Digital Fluency

Woven into Every Session

Building Confident, Capable Browser Skills

Digital fluency is not a prerequisite — it is a companion. Rather than front-loading technical skills before the real work begins, fluency is introduced progressively, in context, precisely when each skill is needed. You will develop browser confidence naturally as you navigate the tools that power your course development.
Tabs vs. Split-Screen Workflows Bookmarks & Navigation Upload & Download Mechanics Screen Management Sharing & Publishing Working with AI Interfaces File Handling
Progressive disclosure rather than prerequisite knowledge — each skill introduced at the moment it becomes useful.
Looking Further Ahead

Deeper AI-Assisted Work

Once syllabi are in place, we will turn to the more expansive dimensions of the workshop objectives:

1
AI-Assisted Topic Research
Using AI to research and enrich the important topics within each individual syllabus.
2
Teaching Visualizations
Creating visualizations that integrate directly into course sessions — tools for teaching, not just planning.
