📝Session Summary — Jun 21, 2026
Date: June 21, 2026 · Format: Hybrid Zoom · Facilitator: John Day · Attendance: TBD

The inaugural AILit SIG session opened with participants reflecting on what drew them to the group — a mix of curiosity, mild anxiety, and the desire to move from passive observers of the AI moment to active participants. The discussion quickly found its stride.

Key Themes
  • The distinction between tool use and genuine understanding — can we be effective users without knowing how the machine works?
  • Trust calibration: how the group currently assesses AI output versus news media versus human experts
  • The live Claude demonstration surfaced the importance of prompt framing — small changes in wording, large changes in result
  • Strong interest in a recurring format centered on immersive reading and focused AI experiments

The session closed with consensus on a fall theme — AI & the Professions — and agreement to circulate a short survey to gauge members' preferred topics and session cadence. The Ed Murrow discussion protocol, developed during the AILIT course, will be adapted for SIG use.

The AILit Mission

Learning by Doing

Every session includes a hands-on element — working with AI tools directly, not just discussing them from a distance.

Critical Engagement

We ask hard questions about AI claims, errors, and epistemology — applying the same standards we use for any knowledge source.

Immersive Reading

Close reading with AI as an active interlocutor — from Shakespeare to news analysis — is a core SIG practice.

Structured Dialogue

The Ed Murrow protocol — developed in the AILit course — shapes our discussions: deliberate, evidence-grounded, multiple voices.

Building Fluency

Moving BOLLI members from passive observers to confident, purposeful users of AI tools — the core goal of the SIG.

Double Resonance

AILit carries two meanings at once: AI Literacy, and the pre-digital lit of the 1960s — a word that still means something.
