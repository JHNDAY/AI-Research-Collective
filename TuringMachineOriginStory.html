üìÖ Late 1936
üéØ Solving the Entscheidungsproblem
Turing uses his machine concept to definitively solve the Entscheidungsproblem:
The Proof Strategy:
  1. Define "algorithmic" as "computable by a Turing Machine"
  2. Show that the "Halting Problem" is undecidable
  3. Reduce the Entscheidungsproblem to the Halting Problem
  4. Therefore: The Entscheidungsproblem is unsolvable
The Revolutionary Result: There is no general algorithm that can determine whether an arbitrary mathematical statement is provable. Mathematics cannot mechanically decide its own truth.
But more importantly, Turing has created the fundamental model of computation itself.
