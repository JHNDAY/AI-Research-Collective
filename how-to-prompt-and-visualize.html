How to Explore a Topic with Claude

The Guide to Narrating a Prompt

A complete guide for creating, visualizing, and organizing your ideas

Transform your thoughts into structured explorations. This tutorial walks you through writing an open-ended prompt, getting Claude's perspective, and creating a beautiful visualization of your findings—all in one seamless workflow.

1
Write a prompt

Start by writing a free-flowing narrative in your preferred writing tool. This isn't a formal prompt—let your thoughts flow naturally.

Be sure to include:

  • What's currently on your mind about the topic
  • Questions you want to explore
  • Context or background that matters
  • Any specific angles you're curious about

Need inspiration? Check out these example prompts to see how others approach open-ended exploration.

2
Paste the prompt into Claude

Copy your written prompt and paste it into a new Claude conversation. Send it as-is—the freeform nature of your writing helps Claude understand your genuine curiosity.

Tip: Don't overthink the phrasing. Claude responds well to authentic, conversational prompts.

3
Review the response

Read through Claude's response carefully. This is your raw material for the next step. Take note of insights, connections, or surprising angles that stand out to you.
