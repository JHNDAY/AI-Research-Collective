Cohort Narrative

This is a predominantly retired, highly educated cohort drawn from careers in law, medicine, education, technology, the arts, social work, publishing, and transportation research. With 23 members, the group continues to grow in professional breadth — notably with Mary Stearns, whose background in transportation and human factors adds a distinctive applied-research perspective, and Marcy, an enthusiastic generalist who already uses AI daily across books, astronomy, and personal curiosity.

Nearly everyone describes themselves as a lifelong learner motivated by a shared desire not to be "left behind" as AI reshapes daily life. The group skews strongly toward the humanities: interests in reading, writing, history, politics, and the arts outnumber purely technical pursuits. Several members are actively pursuing new chapters — a Dante course website, art museum docent work, course development, original writing — and see AI as a practical creative tool rather than an end in itself.

AI experience ranges from genuine apprehension (one member cites "psychological avoidance"; another says she "fears it rather than takes advantage of it") to confident daily use (Barbara Kriss uses it for everything from literary to medical questions). This spectrum means the cohort benefits from foundational literacy sessions alongside applied, project-based work. The strong Turner Street interest (61%) reflects healthy appetite for in-person collaboration.
