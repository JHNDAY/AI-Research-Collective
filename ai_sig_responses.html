Cohort Narrative

This is a predominantly retired, highly educated cohort drawn from careers in law, medicine, education, technology, the arts, social work, publishing, science, real estate, and international education. With 43 members, the group's most recent additions include Aaron Goldberg, an electronic engineer, and John Rudy, an MIT graduate who spent decades as a Raytheon director — both joining the cohort's now-largest Technology & Engineering sector — alongside Susan Wagner, a retired consumer goods marketing and analytics executive, and Narciso Tan, a retired part-time worker curious about AI and technology generally.

Nearly everyone describes themselves as a lifelong learner motivated by a shared desire not to be "left behind" as AI reshapes daily life. The group skews strongly toward the humanities and public service: interests in reading, writing, history, the arts, and volunteer work outnumber purely technical pursuits, even as the Technology & Engineering sector has grown into the cohort's single largest professional category. Several members are pursuing concrete personal projects — a Dante course website, a book on notable Newton women, a novel-in-progress, museum docent work, organizing an AI assistant for daily life, even keeping houseplants healthy (Beth Polebaum's stated motivation) — and see AI as a practical creative and organizational tool rather than an end in itself.

AI experience ranges from genuine apprehension (one member cites "psychological avoidance"; another says she "fears it rather than takes advantage of it") to confident, multi-tool daily use — several members now specifically reference using Claude alongside ChatGPT. This spectrum means the cohort benefits from foundational literacy sessions alongside applied, project-based work. Turner Street interest holds at 58%, reflecting continued appetite for in-person collaboration even as the group has grown to 43.
