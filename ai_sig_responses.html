Cohort Narrative

This is a predominantly retired, highly educated cohort drawn from careers in law, medicine, education, technology, the arts, and social services. Nearly everyone describes themselves as a lifelong learner motivated by a shared desire not to be "left behind" as AI reshapes daily life — a phrase that appears, in some form, in nearly a third of responses.

The group skews toward the humanities: interests in reading, writing, history, politics, and the arts outnumber purely technical pursuits. Several members are actively pursuing new chapters — a Dante course website, art museum docent work, course development — and see AI as a practical tool for those creative projects rather than an end in itself.

AI experience ranges from genuine apprehension (one member cites "psychological avoidance"; another says she "fears it rather than takes advantage of it") to confident weekly use. This spread means the cohort can benefit from both foundational literacy work and applied project-based sessions. The high Turner Street attendance interest (63%) suggests strong appetite for in-person collaboration.
