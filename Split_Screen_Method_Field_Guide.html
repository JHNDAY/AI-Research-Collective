The Split‑Screen Method

in the same browser window — for Chrome and Firefox

Your draft on one side. Claude coaching you on the other. Set it up once, and the two never have to trade places in a single tab again.

Your draft Your coach
Split View, realized — jhnday.github.io/AI‑Research‑Collective/ailit_portal

Most of us write the way we always have: draft in one window, alt‑tab to Claude, copy a paragraph over, read the feedback, tab back, and try to hold onto what it said. Firefox's Split View skips all of that. Set it up once, and your draft and your coach sit beside each other for the rest of the session.

Three steps get you there.

Everything starts here

Open the AILit portal — jhnday.github.io/AI‑Research‑Collective/ailit_portal — and look for the Resource Bar along the bottom.

01
✓ new tab opened
Step One

Open Claude

In the Resource Bar, click Claude. A fresh tab opens beside the portal, ready for a new conversation.

02
✓ new tab opened
Step Two

Open the Writing Coach

Click back to the portal tab, scroll to Session Materials, and click Writing Coach. It opens in a tab of its own — the Narrative Prompt Coach page, ready to guide you.

03
3a — right-click the Writing Coach tab
3b — choose the Claude tab
Step Three

Snap them together

Right‑click the Writing Coach tab and choose Add Split View. Firefox asks which tab to pair it with — pick New chat – Claude.

And that's it. You're looking at the screen from the top of this page: your draft on the left, your coach on the right, for as long as the session lasts.

A note on browsers

This walkthrough uses Firefox's built‑in Split View. If you're in Chrome or Edge, look for a similar side‑by‑side or "split screen" option — or simply open two browser windows and place them next to each other on your screen. Either way, the goal is the same: your draft and your coach, both visible, all session long.
