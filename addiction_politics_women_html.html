Addiction Patterns and Political Shifts Among Young Women (2014-2024)

Exploring correlations between substance/behavioral addictions and liberal political alignment

Addiction Rates vs Political Shift (Young Women 18-29)

Key Observations

  • Opposite trend to men: Higher addiction rates correlate with increased liberal political alignment
  • Social media addiction shows the strongest correlation with liberal shifts (-0.78)
  • Eating disorders also strongly correlate with liberal political views
  • Young women moved more liberal over the decade, opposite to young men's conservative shift

Correlation Analysis: Addiction Types vs Liberal Shift

Strongest Correlations (Liberal Direction)

Social Media (r=-0.78): Exposure to diverse perspectives and social justice content
Eating Disorders (r=-0.65): Body image issues linked to feminist and body positivity movements
Opioid Use (r=-0.42): Healthcare access concerns driving political engagement

Potential Mechanisms

• Social awareness through digital platforms
• Healthcare advocacy from personal struggles
• Feminist perspectives on addiction stigma
• Community support in progressive spaces
